[^1]: Septimius Severus (193-211 CE) founded the Severan dynasty and strengthened the empire's borders through successful military campaigns. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Septimius_Severus)

[^2]: Antoninus Pius (138-161 CE) presided over a peaceful reign marked by economic prosperity and no major wars. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoninus_Pius)

[^3]: Maxentius (306-312 CE) ruled as a usurper in Rome and Italy, maintaining the old Roman religious traditions until his defeat by Constantine at the Battle of Milvian Bridge. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maxentius)

[^4]: Constantine I (306-337 CE) reunified the empire, established Constantinople as the new capital, and was the first emperor to convert to Christianity. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constantine_the_Great)

[^5]: Titus (79-81 CE) completed the Colosseum and helped the people during the eruption of Mount Vesuvius, earning popularity during his brief reign. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titus)

[^6]: Nerva (96-98 CE) began the line of the Five Good Emperors, establishing the practice of adopting a capable heir rather than relying on hereditary succession. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nerva)

[^7]: Augustus (27 BCE - 14 CE) established and became the first Emperor of the Roman Empire, transforming Rome from a republic into the most powerful empire in the ancient world. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augustus)

[^8]: Trajan (98-117 CE) expanded the Roman Empire to its greatest territorial extent and was remembered as one of Rome's finest rulers. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trajan)

[^9]: Julius Caesar (49-44 BCE) transformed the Roman Republic through civil war and became dictator perpetuo, setting the stage for imperial rule before his assassination on the Ides of March. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julius_Caesar)

[^10]: Marcus Aurelius (161-180 CE) was the philosopher emperor who wrote "Meditations" while defending the empire against Germanic tribes. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcus_Aurelius)

[^11]: Commodus (180-192 CE) ended the era of the Five Good Emperors, known for his megalomaniacal behavior and fighting as a gladiator. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commodus)

[^12]: In Roman mythology, Romulus and Remus are twin brothers whose story tells of the events that led to the founding of the city of Rome and the Roman Kingdom by Romulus, following his fratricide of Remus.
